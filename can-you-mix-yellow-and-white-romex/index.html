White Romex ( Properties, Usage, Performance)

ROMEX® white is a type of Non-metallic (NM) sheathed cable that is commonly used in residential wiring applications. It is made by Southwire and consists of two or three Thhn wires and a ground wire. The white ROMEX® has the lowest amperage available, with a load capacity of 15 amps.

It is typically used for outlets, lights, and smoke detectors in the home.

Denoted as 14-2

One of the main properties of white ROMEX® is its 14-gauge size, which is denoted as 14-2 when there are two insulated wire conductors and 14-3 when there are three wires.

This gauge size is relatively thin, which makes the wire flexible and easy to work with. However, it also means that the wire has a lower capacity for handling electrical current.

Usage

In terms of usage, white ROMEX® is suitable for most standard electrical installations in the home. It can be used to wire outlets, lights, and other small appliances that do not require a large amount of electrical power.

It is also often used to connect smoke detectors, as the 15-amp rating is sufficient for powering these devices.

Performance

In terms of performance, white ROMEX® is generally reliable and effective for its intended purposes. It is designed to safely carry electrical current and has undergone testing to ensure it meets industry standards.

As long as it is installed correctly and not overloaded, white ROMEX® should perform well and provide reliable power to the devices it is connected to.

Yellow Romex ( Properties, Usage, Performance)

ROMEX® yellow is a type of Non-metallic (NM) sheathed cable that is commonly used in residential wiring applications. It is made by Southwire and consists of two or three Thhn wires and a ground wire.

The yellow ROMEX® has a higher amperage rating than the white ROMEX®, with a load capacity of 20 amps. It is typically used for larger appliances, such as microwaves or welders, that require more electrical power.

Denoted as 12-2

This gauge size is relatively thick, which makes the wire more durable and able to handle higher electrical currents. However, it also makes the wire less flexible and more difficult to work with.

Usage

In terms of usage, yellow ROMEX® is suitable for larger electrical installations in the home that require a higher amperage rating. It can be used to wire receptacles, three-way circuits, and appliances that need a higher electrical power supply.

It is also often used in outdoor installations, as the thicker gauge of the wire makes it more resistant to damage from weather and other external factors.

Performance

In terms of performance, yellow ROMEX® is generally reliable and effective for its intended purposes. It is designed to safely carry higher electrical currents and has undergone testing to ensure it meets industry standards.

As long as it is installed correctly and not overloaded, yellow ROMEX® should perform well and provide a strong and reliable power supply to the devices it is connected to.

Can You Mix Yellow and White Romex?

It is generally not recommended to mix different gauges of wire on the same circuit. This can cause confusion for future electricians working on the circuit, and it may violate local building codes. Additionally, using a higher gauge wire (e.g. 12 gauge) on a circuit with a lower amperage rating (e.g. 15 amps) will not provide any benefits and may actually cause problems, such as overloading the circuit. It is best to use the same gauge wire throughout a circuit to avoid potential issues.

Why You Can’t Mix the White and Yellow Romex? What Will Be the Problem?

Mixing white and yellow ROMEX® cables can cause several problems, including:

  1. Confusion for electricians: Using different gauges of wire on the same circuit can make it difficult for electricians to determine the correct wire size and amperage rating when working on the circuit in the future. This can lead to mistakes and potentially dangerous situations.
  2. Violation of building codes: In many cases, building codes specify the minimum gauge size that must be used for different types of electrical installations. Mixing wire gauges on the same circuit may violate these codes, which can result in fines or other penalties.
  3. Overloading the circuit: Using a lower gauge wire (e.g. white ROMEX®) on a circuit with a higher amperage rating (e.g. yellow ROMEX®) can cause the circuit to become overloaded. This can result in electrical fires, damaged equipment, and other hazards.
  4. Reduced efficiency: Mixing wire gauges on the same circuit can reduce the overall efficiency of the circuit. This is because the lower gauge wire may not be able to handle the electrical current being supplied by the higher gauge wire, which can cause voltage drop and other issues.
  5. Increased resistance: Using different gauges of wire on the same circuit can increase the overall resistance of the circuit. This can reduce the amount of electrical power that is available to the devices connected to the circuit, which can lead to poor performance and reduced efficiency.
  6. Poor aesthetics: Mixing white and yellow ROMEX® cables can result in an unsightly and unprofessional-looking installation. This can be a potential turn-off for homeowners or other potential buyers of the property, which can affect the value and marketability of the property.
