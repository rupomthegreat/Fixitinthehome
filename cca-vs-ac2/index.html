What Is CCA?

CCA, or Chromated Copper Arsenate, is a type of wood preservative. It has been widely used in the past to protect the wood from pests and decay. It is made up of a mixture of copper, chromium, and arsenic. These are toxic to pests and fungi that can damage the wood.

When CCA is applied to wood, it penetrates the surface and reacts with the wood cells. This creates a barrier that is toxic to pests and fungi. This barrier helps to prevent wood rot and insect infestation. It can significantly extend the lifespan of wood structures.

CCA has been in use since the 1930s. It was once the most widely used wood preservative in the world. It was particularly popular for use in outdoor structures like decks, fences, and playground equipment. It was also used for marine and industrial applications.

However, in recent years, concerns about the potential health risks of arsenic exposure have led to a decline in the use of CCA. This has also led to the development of safer alternatives. Despite this, CCA-treated lumber is still available for purchase in some areas.

Concerns About CCA

One of the main concerns about CCA is the potential health risks associated with arsenic exposure. Arsenic is a known carcinogen. Long-term exposure to arsenic has been linked to an increased risk of cancer, cardiovascular disease, and other health problems.

In addition, arsenic can be toxic to the nervous system. High levels of exposure can cause symptoms like tremors, weakness, and numbness.

Given the potential health risks of arsenic exposure, there have been efforts in recent years to phase out the use of CCA. There have also been efforts to find safer alternatives.

In 2003, the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) issued a voluntary cancellation of the registration for CCA for use in most residential wood products. This was due to concerns about the potential risks of arsenic exposure to children. Despite this, CCA-treated lumber is still available for purchase in some areas. It is also still used in certain industrial and marine applications.
