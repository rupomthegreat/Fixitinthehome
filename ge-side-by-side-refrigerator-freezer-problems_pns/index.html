Recall of Ge French Door Refrigerators With Bottom Freezers

In April 2022, GE Appliances, a Haier Company, issued a recall for six models of its GE-brand French Door Refrigerators with bottom freezers in fingerprint-resistant stainless steel. The affected refrigerators were manufactured from February 2020 to August 2021 and sold at U.S.

home improvement and appliance stores including Best Buy, Home Depot, and Lowe’s. The recall was initiated due to potential fire hazards.

Models Affected by the Recall

The following six models are affected by the recall:

  • GNE27JSMSS
  • GNE27JSKSS
  • GNE27ESMSS
  • GNE27ESKSS
  • GNE27EBMES
  • GNE27EBKES

Consumers can determine if their refrigerator is part of the recall by checking the brand name, model, and serial number located on a label inside the refrigerator compartment.

Potential Fire Hazards

According to the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC), the recall was initiated because the refrigerators can overheat, posing a fire hazard. GE has received several reports of the refrigerators overheating and causing fires, although no injuries have been reported.

Steps for Recall Resolution

GE is providing a free repair for all affected refrigerators. Consumers are urged to immediately stop using the affected refrigerators and unplug them. GE will provide a free repair to remedy the potential fire hazard, which includes installing a new main control board.

Consumers can contact GE for a free repair by calling 833-450-2184 or visiting GE’s recall website.

the recall of GE French Door Refrigerators with Bottom Freezers highlights the importance of regularly checking for product recalls, especially for home appliances that can pose a safety risk. Consumers who own an affected refrigerator should take immediate action and follow the steps outlined by GE for a free repair to ensure the safety of their home and family.

Common Problems With Ge Side-by-side Refrigerators and Freezers

Not Cooling Properly

One of the most common problems with GE side-by-side refrigerators and freezers is that they may not cool properly. This issue can be caused by several factors, including the unit not being plugged in properly, the door not closing tightly, broken seals, clogged vents, or an unclean or broken condenser. In some cases, the issue can also be caused by a switch that has been turned off, or the plug being incorrectly lodged inside the socket.

Door Not Closing Tightly

Another common problem is that the door may not close tightly, which can cause the refrigeration system to work harder than it should. This, in turn, can cause the unit to not cool as effectively, and can also result in higher energy bills. To prevent this issue, it is important to ensure that the door is closing properly and to check the gaskets regularly for damage or wear.

Broken Seals

Broken seals are another common problem with GE side-by-side refrigerators and freezers. This can occur for several reasons, including overloading the unit with too much food, using the door too frequently, or using improper cleaning methods. To avoid this issue, it is important to not overfill the unit, to minimize the frequency of opening and closing the door, and to clean the unit properly using a gentle cleaning solution.

Clogged Vents

Clogged vents are another common problem with GE side-by-side refrigerators and freezers. This can occur due to a build-up of dust, dirt, and other debris inside the unit. To avoid this issue, it is important to regularly clean the vents and the interior of the unit.

Unclean or Broken Condensers

The condenser is an important part of the refrigeration system, and it can become unclean or broken over time. When this occurs, the unit may not cool properly and may even stop working entirely. To avoid this issue, it is important to clean the condenser regularly and to have it checked by a professional if it appears to be damaged or not functioning properly.

Switches Turned Off or Plugs Lodged Incorrectly

In some cases, the unit may not be working properly due to a switch being turned off or the plug being lodged incorrectly. To avoid this issue, it is important to check the switches and the plug regularly, and to ensure that they are in the correct position and properly connected.

it is important to be aware of the common problems that can occur with GE side-by-side refrigerators and freezers, in order to prevent them and to ensure that the unit continues to function properly. If you encounter any issues with your unit, it is important to have it checked by a professional and to take appropriate steps to resolve the problem.

Recommendations for Fixing Common Problems
