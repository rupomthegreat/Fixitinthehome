Toro Red Paint Equivalent

There are two different shades of Toro red paint: the “old” apple red and the “new” Toro red.

Toro p/n 361-1 is the old apple red, and Toro p/n 361-10 is the new Toro red. Rust-Oleum Sunrise Red has been used as a match for the new Toro red, while Krylon Banner Red is a direct match for the old apple red.

Rust-Oleum High Performance Enamel “Bright Red” is also a close match. POR-15 makes an engine enamel called “Chevrolet Red” that is a dead ringer for Toro’s apple red and is extremely durable. You can also get Toro touch-up paint directly from Toro.

Types of Toro Red Paint

Toro’s signature Toro New Red paint color has undergone a few changes over the years, and as a result, there are two different types of Toro red paint. Toro’s original red paint color was slightly more orange-toned and referred to as “Toro Red” or “Old Toro Red.” In 1997, Toro introduced a new, deeper red paint color that was dubbed “Toro New Red.”

Toro’s New Red paint color is a darker, richer red shade that closely resembles candy apple red. It has a more modern and sleek look than the older Toro Red color. In contrast, Toro’s old red color is slightly more orange-toned and brighter than the newer version.

Tips for Painting Toro Equipment

If you’re planning to paint your Toro equipment, here are some tips to ensure a smooth and long-lasting finish:

Clean the Surface

Before painting, ensure the surface is clean and free of debris, dirt, and rust. Use a wire brush or sandpaper to remove any rust or peeling paint, then wipe the surface with a cloth or tack cloth to remove any dust.

Apply Primer

Applying a primer to the surface will help the paint adhere better and last longer. Use a primer that is compatible with your chosen paint type and allow it to dry completely.

Choose the Right Paint

It’s essential to select a paint that is specifically designed for outdoor use and can withstand the elements, such as the Toro red paint. If you’re not able to purchase the Toro red paint, choose an equivalent color from a reputable brand.

Apply the Paint

Apply the paint in thin, even coats, and allow it to dry between coats. Ensure that you’re painting in a well-ventilated area, and wear protective equipment such as a mask and gloves.

Add Gloss

Applying a gloss finish will provide an extra layer of protection to your equipment and make it easier to clean, especially during heavy or wet snow. Glossy paint has a smooth surface, which reduces the amount of snow that sticks to the surface.
