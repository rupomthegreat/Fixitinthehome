Door Too Wide for Frame [What to Do]

Edited byMinhazul Abedin December 25, 2022January 3, 2025

Having a door that is too wide for your door frame can be frustrating and inconvenient. It may cause the door to stick or not close properly, and it can also be unsightly. This issue is especially common in older homes, where the rough opening may not be the correct size for modern doors.

If you find yourself in this situation, don’t worry – there are several options available to help you get your door fitting properly in the frame. In this article, we’ll explore some of the causes of a door being too wide for the frame, and outline some options for fixing it.

Causes of a Door Being Too Wide for the Frame

There are a few different reasons why a door might be too wide for the frame.

Small Rough Opening

One of the most common causes is that the rough opening (the opening in the wall where the door is installed) is too small. This can happen when the door frame was originally installed incorrectly or if the house has settled over time, causing the rough opening to become narrower.

Door Too Wide

Another cause of a door being too wide for the frame is that the door itself was mismeasured. This can happen if the person installing the door measured the rough opening incorrectly or if the door was ordered in the wrong size.

Manufacturing Fault

In very few cases, the door may have been made slightly too wide, either by mistake or due to variations in the manufacturing process. Regardless of the cause, there are several options available to help you get your door fitting properly in the frame.

Options to Fix a Door that is Too Wide for the Frame [Or Small]

If you have a door that is too wide for the door frame, there are a few options you can consider:

  1. Shave down the door: This involves taking the door apart and removing a small amount of material from the edge of the door that is too wide. You can use a hand plane, block plane, or belt sander to remove the excess material, but be careful to keep the edge straight and even.
  2. Modify the door frame: If the door frame is too narrow, you can try widening it by adding a strip of wood to the side of the frame that is too narrow. This can be done by removing the trim, adding the wood strip, and then reattaching the trim.
  3. Use shims when the door is too small: Shims are thin wedges of wood that can be used to fill gaps between the door and the frame. If the door is slightly too wide, you can use shims to fill the gap and help the door fit properly.
  4. Use a door sweep when gap beneath the door: A door sweep is a strip of rubber or silicone that is attached to the bottom of the door. It seals the gap between the door and the floor and can help reduce drafts and improve the door’s overall fit.

Ultimately, the best solution will depend on the specifics of your situation, including the size of the door, the size of the frame, and the tools you have available.

How to Shave Down the Door to Fit in the Frame?

Tools needed:

  • Screwdriver
  • Tape measure
  • Workbench
  • Hand plane, block plane, or belt sander
  • Straight edge
  • Pencil
  • Clamp
  • Sandpaper block or belt sander
  • Damp cloth

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to shave down a door to fit in the frame:

  1. Remove the door from the hinges: To shave down the door, you will need to remove it from the hinges. Use a screwdriver to remove the screws that hold the hinges to the door and the frame.
  2. Measure the gap between the door and the frame: Use a tape measure to determine how much material needs to be removed from the door. Make a mark on the door at the point where it needs to be trimmed.
  3. Set up a workbench: To shave down the door, you will need a workbench with a sturdy surface and good lighting. Set up your workbench in a well-ventilated area, away from any heat sources or open flames.
  4. Choose the right tool: There are a few different tools you can use to shave down a door, including a hand plane, block plane, or belt sander. Choose the tool that you feel most comfortable using, and make sure it is sharp and in good working order.
  5. Mark the cutting line: Use a straight edge and a pencil to mark a cutting line on the door, following the mark you made in step 2. Make sure the line is straight and even.
  6. Clamp the door to the workbench: Use a clamp to secure the door to the workbench, with the cutting line facing up. Make sure the door is stable and won’t move while you are working on it.
  7. Start shaving down the door: Hold the tool steady and use long, even strokes to remove the excess material from the door. Work your way from one end of the door to the other, following the cutting line as closely as possible. Use a light touch to avoid removing too much material at once.
  8. Check your progress: As you work, check your progress frequently by comparing the door to the frame. If the door still doesn’t fit properly, continue shaving down the door until it fits properly.
  9. Sand the door: Once you have shaved down the door to the desired size, use a sandpaper block or belt sander to smooth out any rough edges or uneven spots. Wipe the door down with a damp cloth to remove any sawdust or debris.
  10. Rehang the door: Once the door is the right size and smooth, reattach it to the hinges and install it in the frame. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure the door fits properly and operates smoothly.

How to Modify the Door Frame to Fit in the Frame?

Tools needed:

  • Tape measure
  • Pry bar or hammer and chisel
  • Saw
  • Wood glue
  • Nails or screws
  • Screwdriver or drill with screw bit
  • Level
  • Pencil
  • Straight edge

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to modify a door frame to fit a door:

  1. Measure the gap between the door and the frame: Use a tape measure to determine how much the frame needs to be modified in order to fit the door properly.
  2. Remove the trim: Use a pry bar or hammer and chisel to remove the trim around the door frame. Be careful not to damage the trim or the wall.
  3. Cut a strip of wood to fit the gap: Use a saw to cut a strip of wood that is slightly wider than the gap between the door and the frame. The strip should be the same thickness as the door frame.
  4. Attach the wood strip to the frame: Use wood glue and nails or screws to attach the wood strip to the frame. Make sure the strip is level and aligned with the rest of the frame.
  5. Reinstall the trim: Once the wood strip is in place, reinstall the trim around the door frame. Use nails or screws to secure the trim in place.
  6. Install the door: Once the frame has been modified, install the door in the frame. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure the door fits properly and operates smoothly.

How to Use Shims to Fit in the Frame when the Door is too Small?

Tools needed:

  • Tape measure
  • Saw or utility knife
  • Hammer
  • Screwdriver or drill with screw bit (if installing door hinges)

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to use shims to fit a door in the frame:

  1. Measure the gap between the door and the frame: Use a tape measure to determine how much the frame needs to be adjusted in order to fit the door properly.
  2. Cut the shims: Use a saw or utility knife to cut the shims to the size and shape needed to fill the gap between the door and the frame.
  3. Place the shims: Place the shims between the door and the frame, using a hammer to gently tap them into place if necessary.
  4. Check the fit: Close the door and check the fit. If the door still doesn’t fit properly, add more shims as needed until the door fits snugly in the frame.
  5. Install the door: Once the shims are in place and the door fits properly, install the door in the frame. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure the door operates smoothly.

How to Use a Door Sweep When there is Gap Beneath the Door?

Tools needed:

  • Tape measure
  • Screwdriver or drill with screw bit (if installing door sweep with screws)
  • Adhesive (if installing door sweep with an adhesive)
  • Screwdriver or drill with screw bit (if installing door hinges)

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to use a door sweep to fit a door in the frame:

  • Measure the gap between the door and the floor: Use a tape measure to determine the size of the gap between the bottom of the door and the floor.
  • Choose a door sweep: Select a door sweep that is the right size and style for your door. There are a few different types of door sweeps, including ones that attach to the bottom of the door and ones that install on the door frame.
  • Install the door sweep: Follow the manufacturer’s instructions to install the door sweep on the door or the door frame. This will typically involve using screws or adhesive to attach the sweep to the door or frame.
  • Adjust the sweep: Once the door sweep is installed, adjust it as needed to ensure it seals properly against the floor. You may need to adjust the sweep up or down or bend it slightly to get the best fit.
  • Install the door: Once the door sweep is in place and the door fits properly, install the door in the frame. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure the door operates smoothly.

Things You Should Keep in Mind

When fixing a wide door, it is important to take a few things into consideration in order to achieve a successful result:

  1. Measure accurately: Before you start any work, make sure you measure the door and the frame accurately. This will help you determine exactly how much material needs to be removed or added and will help you avoid making mistakes that could compromise the door’s fit or function.
  2. Use the right tools: Choose the right tools for the job, and make sure they are in good working order. Using the wrong tools, or tools that are damaged or dull can make the job more difficult and may result in poor-quality work.
  3. Take your time: Rushing through the job can lead to mistakes and poor-quality work. Take your time and focus on doing the job correctly, rather than trying to finish as quickly as possible.
  4. Follow safety guidelines: Follow all safety guidelines and precautions when working on the door. This may include wearing protective gear, such as gloves and safety glasses, and using tools in the proper manner.
  5. Test the door’s fit and function: After completing the work, test the door to make sure it fits properly and operates smoothly. If the door still doesn’t fit properly or has problems operating, you may need to make additional adjustments or seek the help of a professional.

Final Words

If you have a door that is too wide for the door frame, there are a few options you can consider in order to fix the problem. These include shaving down the door, modifying the door frame, using shims, or installing a door sweep.

Each of these approaches has its own set of advantages and disadvantages, and the best solution will depend on the specifics of your situation. It is important to measure accurately, use the right tools, take your time, and follow safety guidelines when working on the door.

After completing the work, test the door to make sure it fits properly and operates smoothly. If you are unsure of the best approach or encounter any problems, it may be a good idea to consult a professional carpenter or home improvement expert.
