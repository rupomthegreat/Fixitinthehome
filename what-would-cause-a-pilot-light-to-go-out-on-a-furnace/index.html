Reasons Why a Pilot Light Go Out

There are several reasons why a pilot light goes out. Some of the most important reasons are described below:

  1. Dirty or malfunctioning thermocouple

Thermocouples are used to measure the temperature of a fuel or air stream. Thermocouples are small metal wires that have a melting point that is different from the surrounding temperature. The thermocouple is connected to an electronic instrument.

  1. The air is getting too cold

A malfunctioning thermostat can result in a furnace that is too cold. The thermostat is a valve that controls the flow of air through the furnace. Usually, the thermostat is located inside the furnace and is controlled by the furnace’s airflow.

  1. The furnace is too hot

Some furnaces are equipped with a thermostat that is controlled by the furnace’s airflow. This thermostat is usually located outside of the furnace. This thermostat is activated by the temperature of the air entering the furnace. When the air temperature is very low, the thermostat will ensure that the airflow is tight and that the furnace is not allowed to heat up too much.

  1. The air filter needs to be cleaned

The air filter is a device that collects particulates and dust from the air that is going into the furnace. Cleaning the air filter makes sure that your furnace will run efficiently and will have the highest efficiency possible.

  1. Insufficient air pressure

The thermostat is usually controlled by the furnace’s air pressure. When the air pressure is too low, the thermostat will not allow the air to flow properly. This will result in the furnace being too hot or too cold. The pilot light might also make a loud sound in some cases.
