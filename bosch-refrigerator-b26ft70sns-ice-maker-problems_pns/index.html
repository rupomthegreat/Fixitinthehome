Bosch Refrigerator B26FT70SNS Ice Maker Problems and Solutions

The Bosch B26FT70SNS refrigerator is a popular appliance known for its sleek design and high-end features. However, like all appliances, it can face some problems, particularly with its ice maker. A functioning ice maker is crucial for any refrigerator, as it provides a convenient source of ice for your drinks and food.

Unfortunately, several users of the Bosch B26FT70SNS have reported issues with their ice makers, ranging from not producing ice to dispensing issues. In this blog, we will explore the common ice maker problems faced by Bosch B26FT70SNS users, as well as potential solutions for these issues.

Bosch Refrigerator B26FT70SNS Ice Maker Problems

The Bosch B26FT70SNS refrigerator is known to have issues with its ice maker. Two common problems are: not producing ice, or producing ice but having trouble dispensing it through the door slot. There may also be a red LED light in the on/off switch inside the ice maker that is solid red.

The On/Off switch can be located by removing the ice bucket or feeling underneath the ice tray or bucket. It takes approximately 18 minutes to produce one ice cube. To reset the ice maker, you may need to call a repair service as there is a potential service bulletin for this issue.

Problem 1: Ice Maker Not Producing Ice

The most obvious symptom of this issue is that the ice maker is not producing any ice, or the amount produced is significantly less than usual. This can be confirmed by checking the ice bin for any ice cubes or observing the behavior of the ice maker itself.

Causes of the Issue

There are several potential causes of this problem, including:

  1. Water Supply: If the water supply to the ice maker is not working properly, it will not be able to produce ice.
  2. Clogged Filter: A clogged filter in the water line can reduce or stop the flow of water to the ice maker, causing it to stop producing ice.
  3. Thermostat: If the thermostat that controls the ice maker is not working properly, it may not be able to produce ice.
  4. Control Module: The control module is responsible for controlling the ice maker, and if it is damaged or not working properly, it will affect the production of ice.

Possible Solutions

  1. Check the water supply: Ensure that the water supply to the ice maker is working properly and there are no leaks.
  2. Replace the filter: If the filter is clogged, replace it with a new one.
  3. Test the thermostat: If you suspect the thermostat is the issue, test it using a multimeter to see if it’s working properly.

Replace the Control Module

If the control module is damaged, it will need to be replaced. You can find the control module for your Bosch B26FT70SNS online or by contacting a professional repair service.

If you’re facing the issue of the ice maker not producing ice in your Bosch B26FT70SNS refrigerator, the first step is to identify the root cause of the issue. With the help of the possible causes and solutions mentioned above, you can get your ice maker working again and enjoy the convenience it provides.

Problem 2: Ice Maker Producing Ice, but Not Dispensing Through Door Slot

The second common issue faced by Bosch B26FT70SNS users is that the ice maker produces ice but does not dispense through the door slot. This can result in an inconvenience as you will have to open the door to retrieve the ice. Some of the symptoms of this issue include:

  • The ice maker produces ice, but it is not dispensed through the door slot
  • Ice is seen inside the ice maker compartment, but not accessible through the door

Causes of the Issue

The most common causes of this issue include:

  • The ice dispenser mechanism is clogged or blocked
  • The ice dispenser mechanism is broken or damaged
  • The ice maker or dispenser motor is not functioning properly
  • The dispenser control module is not working properly

Possible Solutions

Here are a few possible solutions to help you fix this issue:

  1. Clean the dispenser mechanism: Make sure the ice dispenser mechanism is clear of any debris or blockages. Clean the mechanism thoroughly to ensure it is functioning properly.
  2. Check for damage: Inspect the ice dispenser mechanism for any visible damage. If there is any damage, it is best to replace the mechanism.
  3. Replace the motor: If the ice dispenser motor is not functioning properly, you may need to replace it. This is a technical job and it is best to seek the assistance of a professional appliance repair service.
  4. Replace the control module: If the dispenser control module is not working properly, you may need to replace it. This is also a technical job and it is best to seek the assistance of a professional appliance repair service.

These are the common symptoms, causes, and possible solutions for the issue of an ice maker producing ice but not dispensing through the door slot in a Bosch B26FT70SNS refrigerator. If the issue persists, it is best to seek the assistance of a professional appliance repair service to ensure proper functioning of your appliance.

Problem 3: Solid Red Led Light in on/off Switch

If the Bosch B26FT70SNS ice maker is having issues, the red LED light by the on/off switch may be solid red. This indicates that there may be a problem with the ice maker.

Causes of the Issue

There are several reasons why the red LED light in the on/off switch of the Bosch B26FT70SNS refrigerator may be solid red, including a malfunctioning ice maker motor, an issue with the ice maker control module, or a problem with the wiring in the ice maker.

Possible Solutions

If the red LED light in the on/off switch is solid red, it is recommended to unplug the refrigerator and troubleshoot the issue. Some possible solutions include checking the wiring and connections, replacing the ice maker motor, or replacing the ice maker control module. If you are unsure about how to resolve the issue, it is best to contact a professional for assistance.

If you are facing a problem with the ice maker in your Bosch B26FT70SNS refrigerator, it is important to identify the symptoms and causes of the issue in order to find a solution. Whether you opt to troubleshoot the issue on your own or seek the assistance of a professional, it is important to ensure that your ice maker is functioning properly in order to enjoy the full functionality of your refrigerator.

Problem 4: Ice Maker Taking Too Long to Produce Ice

  • The Bosch B26FT70SNS ice maker is taking longer than expected to produce ice
  • This issue can be noticeable if the user is waiting for ice, but it takes an unusually long time to get a cube.

Causes of the Issue

  1. Low water pressure: If the water pressure to the ice maker is low, it can cause it to take longer to fill the tray and freeze the ice cubes.
  2. Clogged filters: If the water filters are clogged, they can reduce the flow of water to the ice maker and cause it to take longer to produce ice.
  3. Improper installation: If the ice maker was not installed properly, it can cause problems with the production of ice, such as taking too long to produce.

Possible Solutions

  1. Check water pressure: To determine if the low water pressure is causing the issue, you can check the water pressure at the nearest faucet. If it is low, you can consider contacting a plumber to resolve the issue.
  2. Clean the water filters: You can check the water filters to see if they need cleaning or replacement. Dirty filters can reduce the flow of water to the ice maker and cause it to take longer to produce ice.
  3. Check the installation: If the ice maker was not installed properly, it can cause problems with the production of ice. Contact a professional to check the installation if necessary.

Problem 5: Difficulty Locating the on/off Switch

One of the common issues faced by Bosch B26FT70SNS refrigerator users is difficulty in locating the On/Off switch for the ice maker. This switch is responsible for turning the ice maker on and off, and without it, the ice maker would not function.

Causes of the Issue

This issue is caused due to the switch being hidden or not clearly visible. In some models, the switch is hidden under the ice tray or bucket, making it difficult for users to locate it.

Possible Solutions

To resolve this issue, users can follow these steps:

  1. Remove the ice bucket and check inside the ice maker for the On/Off switch.
  2. If the switch is not visible, feel underneath the ice tray or bucket as some models have the switch hidden there.
  3. If the switch still cannot be found, consult the user manual for the specific model for more information.
  4. If the manual does not provide enough information, contact Bosch customer service for assistance.

By following these steps, users should be able to locate the On/Off switch and resolve the issue of difficulty finding it. However, if the switch cannot be found, it is recommended to seek professional assistance to avoid damaging the refrigerator or the ice maker.

Problem 6: Ice Maker Making No Ice at All

This issue occurs when the ice maker is not producing any ice at all. Depending on the model and the reason why the ice maker is not producing ice, the user may experience the issues of the ice maker not producing ice at all or the ice maker not working properly.

Causes of the Issue

There are several reasons why the ice maker may not be working properly, including:

  1. The water supply has been turned off and the ice maker cannot produce ice
  2. The wiring or control module are not working properly
  3. The power supply of the ice maker is not strong enough to produce ice
  4. The control panel is no longer functioning properly
  5. The ice tray in the ice maker is no longer in working condition

If the water supply has been turned off, the ice maker cannot produce ice until it is turned on again

Possible Solutions

If the ice maker is not producing ice due to a power issue, here are a few possible solutions to consider:

  1. Check the wiring: Check the wiring to the ice maker for any possible breakages or damage. Any issues with the wiring or electrical components can cause the ice maker not to produce ice.
  2. Check the control panel: If the power supply is not working properly, it can cause issues with the ice maker, such as not producing ice. Check the control panel to ensure it is in working condition and that it is receiving power from the main panel.
  3. Check the ice tray: If the ice tray is not in working condition, it can result in a similar issue as when the water supply is turned off. If the tray is broken, it will not produce ice.
  4. Consult a technician: If the problems with the ice maker persist and you have no solutions, contact a technician to inspect the components and resolve the issue.

To learn how to fix the issue of a Bosch B26FT70SNS ice maker not producing ice at all, you can always consult Bosch customer service for assistance.

These are the most common issues that users experience with the ice maker, along with possible solutions.

Conclusion

Bosch B26FT70SNS refrigerator users may face several common ice maker problems, such as ice maker not producing ice, ice cubes not dispensing through the door slot, a solid red LED light in the On/Off switch, ice maker taking too long to produce ice, and difficulty locating the On/Off switch.

These problems can be resolved with simple troubleshooting steps, such as checking for blockages in the dispenser, cleaning the ice maker, and resetting the switch. However, it is important to address ice maker problems promptly to ensure the efficient functioning of the refrigerator.
