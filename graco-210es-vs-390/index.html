Greco Pro210ES vs. Graco 390

Edited byMinhazul Abedin December 14, 2022December 14, 2022

When it comes to residential painting jobs, having the right tools and equipment is crucial for achieving professional-grade results. Electric airless sprayers are a popular choice among painting contractors and remodelers, as they offer a fast and efficient way to apply a wide range of coatings.

In this article, we will compare two electric airless sprayers – the Greco 390 PC and the Pro210ES – and highlight their key features, as well as their differences and strengths. This will help you decide which sprayer is best suited for your needs and applications.

Greco 390 PC Electric Airless Sprayer

The 390 PC Electric Airless Sprayer is a lightweight and portable option for residential jobs. It features a durable Endurance Chromex Piston Pump that lasts twice as long as the next leading brand. The sprayer also has a Long-Life V-Max Blue Packing, a Chromex rod, and a hardened stainless steel cylinder for added durability.

Additionally, the 390 PC has a Faster cleaning with the QuikAccess intake valve and an Advantage Drive for the longest-lasting drive. The sprayer also comes with a ProConnect One-Part Pump Replacement System for easy maintenance and reduced downtime. It also has an Easy Out Pump Filter for easy cleaning and less clogging.

The sprayer is built with a rugged steel frame and a 4 leg stand for greater stability. It also has a Variable Pressure Control with a position indicator for repeatable settings. The sprayer comes with a Swivel Inlet Suction Hose for easy access to paint buckets. It also includes a Contractor FTx Airless Spray Gun with standard features and an economic choice for everyday use.

Greco Pro210ES Electric Airless Sprayer

The Pro210ES Electric Airless Sprayer is a durable and reliable option for painting contractors and remodelers. It features a proven Endurance pump that delivers maximum life and supports a .021-inch tip for spraying heavier materials and hot solvents.

The ProConnect System allows for quick and easy pump replacement, eliminating downtime and extending the life of the sprayer. The sprayer is also built with a DuraDC motor that supports longer extension cords, as well as a lightweight design with an offset rubber-grip handle for ease of use.

It comes with a chrome-plated stand that is easy to clean and durable, and a flexible suction hose that allows for direct spraying from 1 or 5-gallon containers. The sprayer also includes an SG3 Metal Spray Gun with a hose swivel for easy handling of longer hose lengths.

It has a TrueAirless Spray Tip with SoftSpray Technology for greater control and less overspray, as well as an Easy Out Pump Filter for added filtration and reduced downtime. The sprayer also has a QuickAccess Inlet Valve for easy removal of debris to prevent downtime.

Greco Pro210ES vs. Graco 390

When comparing the 390 PC Electric Airless Sprayer and the Pro210ES Electric Airless Sprayer, there are a few key differences to consider.

Pump

The 390 PC has a durable Endurance Chromex Piston Pump that lasts twice as long as the next leading brand, providing reliable performance on the job. The Pro210ES also has a proven Endurance pump, but it is designed to support a .021-inch tip for spraying heavier materials and hot solvents, making it better suited for tackling these types of applications.

Design

The 390 PC is lightweight and portable with an offset rubber-grip handle for added comfort and control. This makes it easy to lift and carry and allows for greater maneuverability on the job. The Pro210ES, on the other hand, is built with a welded, chrome-plated steel frame for enhanced durability. This makes it better suited for tougher Jobsite environments, but may be less portable and comfortable to use.

Suction Hose

Both sprayers come with a flexible suction hose for easy access to paint buckets, but the Pro210ES also has a hose swivel on the included SG3 Metal Spray Gun for easy handling of longer hose lengths. This added feature provides greater convenience and flexibility when using the sprayer.

Valves

Both sprayers have a QuickAccess Inlet Valve for easy removal of debris, as well as an Easy Out Pump Filter for reduced clogging and easier maintenance. These features help to prevent downtime and ensure a smooth, high-quality finish.

Stands

The 390 PC has a 4-leg stand for greater stability, while the Pro210ES has a chrome-plated stand that is easy to clean and durable. Both stands provide a solid foundation for the sprayer, but the Pro210ES stand may be more durable and resistant to tough job site conditions.

Overall, both sprayers are reliable and versatile options for residential painting jobs, but the Pro210ES may be better suited for tackling heavier materials and hot solvents, while the 390 PC is a more lightweight and portable option.

Tips for Using the Graco Sprayers

Here are some tips for using the 390 PC and Pro210ES Electric Airless Sprayers:

  1. Always wear protective gear such as goggles, gloves, and a respirator when using the sprayer.
  2. Make sure to read the user manual and follow the instructions carefully to avoid damage to the sprayer or injuries.
  3. When using the 390 PC, be sure to only use water-based coatings such as latex and acrylics. Using solvent-based materials may damage the sprayer.
  4. For the Pro210ES, be sure to use the correct tip size for the material and application being sprayed. Using the wrong tip size can result in poor performance and poor finish quality.
  5. Before using the sprayer, make sure to clean and maintain it according to the manufacturer’s instructions. This will help to prevent clogging and ensure smooth operation.
  6. When using the sprayer, make sure to hold it at a consistent distance from the surface being sprayed. This will help to achieve an even and consistent finish.
  7. If the sprayer begins to clog or lose pressure, stop using it immediately and troubleshoot the problem. Continuing to use a clogged or malfunctioning sprayer can result in poor performance and damage to the sprayer.

Frequently Asked Questions

  1. Can the 390 PC Electric Airless Sprayer be used for commercial painting jobs?

No, the 390 PC is specifically designed for residential painting jobs. It may not have the necessary power and capabilities for larger commercial applications.

  1. Does the Pro210ES Electric Airless Sprayer come with a warranty?

It is not specified in the provided information, but it is common for electric airless sprayers to come with a manufacturer’s warranty. It is recommended to check with the manufacturer or retailer for more information.

  1. Can the 390 PC Electric Airless Sprayer be used with solvent-based materials?

No, the 390 PC is designed for use with water-based coatings such as latex and acrylics. It may not be suitable for use with solvent-based materials.

  1. Is the Pro210ES Electric Airless Sprayer easy to clean and maintain?

Yes, the Pro210ES has an Easy Out Pump Filter and a QuickAccess Inlet Valve for easy cleaning and maintenance. This helps to prevent downtime and ensure a smooth, high-quality finish. You can use it while finishing your basement with low-air ducts.

  1. Does the 390 PC Electric Airless Sprayer have variable pressure control?

Yes, the 390 PC has a Variable Pressure Control with a position indicator for repeatable settings. This allows you to adjust the pressure to suit the specific application and material being sprayed.

  1. Can the Pro210ES Electric Airless Sprayer be used with a long extension cord?

Yes, the Pro210ES is built with a DuraDC Motor that supports longer extension cords. This allows you to use the sprayer in locations where a power outlet may not be easily accessible.

  1. Is the 390 PC Electric Airless Sprayer suitable for use with heavy materials?

No, the 390 PC is designed for use with lighter materials such as latex and acrylics. It may not be suitable for use with heavier materials.

Final Words

The 390 PC and Pro210ES Electric Airless Sprayers are reliable and versatile options for residential painting jobs. The 390 PC is a lightweight and portable sprayer that is easy to use and maintain, making it ideal for residential applications. The Pro210ES, on the other hand, is built with a durable steel frame and supports a .021-inch tip for spraying heavier materials and hot solvents.
Both sprayers have features such as QuickAccess Inlet Valves and Easy Out Pump Filters for easy cleaning and maintenance, as well as variable pressure control for consistent results. Overall, both sprayers are reliable and efficient options for residential painting jobs, and choosing the right one will depend on your specific needs and applications.
