Ferris Vs Scag [Which One is Better?]

Both companies offer a wide range of products that are designed to meet the needs of professional landscapers and groundskeepers. In this comparison, we will take a closer look at the features and benefits of Ferris and Scag mowers to help you make an informed decision when choosing the right mower for your business.

Design and Features

Design and features are essential factors to consider when choosing a commercial mower brand. In this section, we will compare the design and features of both Ferris and Scag brands to help you make an informed decision.

Ferris mowers are designed to offer superior comfort and stability, with their signature suspension system. Ferris mowers are fitted with independent suspension technology, providing the operator with a smoother ride, even on rough terrain.

This feature reduces operator fatigue and enables them to mow for longer periods without feeling exhausted. Ferris mowers also feature a pivoting front axle and a foot-operated deck lift system, providing users with greater control over the machine.

On the other hand, Scag mowers are designed to deliver maximum cutting power and speed. The brand features mowers with various deck sizes, making them ideal for commercial and residential properties. Scag mowers come with dual hydrostatic drive systems, which provide superior control and speed.

The brand’s Velocity Plus cutter deck is designed to deliver a quality cut while the mower is moving at high speeds. Scag mowers also feature an easy-to-use, foot-operated deck lift system that allows for quick and easy adjustments.

One of the most significant differences between Ferris and Scag mowers is their suspension systems. Ferris mowers come equipped with independent suspension technology, providing a smoother ride and reducing operator fatigue.

Scag mowers, on the other hand, come with an adjustable suspension seat that can be customized to fit the operator’s preferences. Additionally, the deck design also differs between the two brands. Ferris mowers have a four-wheel suspension system, while Scag mowers have a fixed front axle.

In conclusion, both Ferris and Scag brands offer excellent commercial mowers with unique designs and features. Ferris mowers are ideal for operators who prioritize comfort and stability, thanks to their independent suspension technology.

Scag mowers are ideal for users who prioritize speed and cutting power, thanks to their dual hydrostatic drive systems and Velocity Plus cutter decks. Ultimately, the choice between Ferris and Scag will depend on your specific needs and preferences.

Cutting Performance

Cutting performance is a critical factor to consider when choosing a commercial mower, and both Ferris and Scag have models that are designed for optimal cutting performance.

Deck design is a crucial factor that affects the cutting performance of mowers, and both Ferris and Scag have unique deck designs. Ferris mowers feature the iCD™ Cutting System, which is designed to create consistent airflow and distribute clippings evenly.

This results in a clean cut and a professional-looking finish. The Ferris iCD™ Cutting System also has a unique greaseable aluminum spindle housing, which improves the longevity of the cutting system.

Scag mowers, on the other hand, feature Velocity Plus™ decks. These decks are designed to reduce the buildup of clippings and improve the overall quality of cut. They have a unique shape that creates a powerful vacuum that sucks the grass blades upward before cutting them.

This unique design ensures a smooth and even cut, even when mowing through thick grass.

Both brands offer a range of deck sizes to cater to different mowing needs. Ferris offers decks ranging from 36 to 72 inches, while Scag offers decks ranging from 36 to 72 inches as well. The choice of deck size will depend on the size of the lawn or property being mowed, as well as the terrain and obstacles present.

In terms of overall cutting performance, both Ferris and Scag offer high-quality mowers that are designed to deliver a superior cut. The choice between the two brands will come down to individual preferences and requirements, as well as the availability of models in the desired size and configuration.

Engine Options

When it comes to engine options, both Ferris and Scag offer a range of choices. Ferris offers a variety of engines from well-known manufacturers such as Briggs & Stratton, Kawasaki, and Vanguard, while Scag offers engines from Kawasaki, Kohler, and Briggs & Stratton. Both brands offer engines with varying horsepower ratings, allowing you to choose the one that best suits your needs.

Horsepower ratings can range from 22 to 37 horsepower for both brands, and the engine power can have a significant impact on the overall performance of the mower. The higher the horsepower rating, the more power the engine can produce, which translates into faster mowing speeds and better cutting performance, especially in heavy grass or uneven terrain. However, more horsepower also means more fuel consumption and a higher purchase price.

It’s important to note that the horsepower rating is not the only factor that determines engine performance. Other factors such as the engine’s torque output and the fuel efficiency of the engine can also affect overall performance. In addition, the engine’s durability and maintenance requirements should also be considered when choosing between different engines.

Overall, both Ferris and Scag offer a variety of engine options to choose from, allowing you to select the one that best fits your specific needs and budget. It’s important to carefully consider the horsepower rating, as well as other factors such as fuel efficiency and durability, to ensure you get the best performance from your commercial mower.

Price

Price is an important factor to consider when choosing between Ferris and Scag mowers. The prices of these two brands can vary greatly depending on the model, features, and location of purchase.

Ferris mowers are generally known to be more expensive than Scag mowers, but they also come with a range of additional features and options that can justify the price difference. Ferris mowers often have more advanced suspension systems and larger deck sizes, as well as a wider range of engine options.

Scag mowers are typically more affordable and are often preferred by those who prioritize simplicity and reliability over advanced features. They are known for their durability and are often used by professionals in the landscaping industry. Scag offers a variety of models, ranging from the entry-level Liberty Z to the top-of-the-line Cheetah.

The prices of both brands are determined by a variety of factors, including the cost of manufacturing, distribution, and marketing. Prices can also vary depending on the location and season of purchase. Additionally, both brands offer financing options that can help make their mowers more accessible to customers.

When comparing the features and performance for the price, it’s important to consider your specific needs and preferences. If you prioritize advanced features and are willing to pay a premium for them, then a Ferris mower may be the better choice.

On the other hand, if you are looking for a more affordable, straightforward option, a Scag mower may be the way to go. It’s also important to consider the long-term costs of ownership, including maintenance, repairs, and fuel consumption, in addition to the initial purchase price.

Maintenance and Durability

Maintenance and durability are important factors to consider when investing in a commercial mower. In this section, we will compare the maintenance and durability of both the Ferris and Scag brands.

Maintenance: Both Ferris and Scag require regular maintenance to keep them running smoothly. This includes changing the oil and air filter, sharpening the blades, and checking the belts and tires. However, the maintenance required for each brand may differ due to the differences in their designs and features.

For example, Ferris mowers have a patented suspension system that requires regular maintenance. On the other hand, Scag mowers have fewer moving parts and require less maintenance.

Durability: Both brands are known for their durability and are built to withstand heavy usage. Ferris mowers have a reputation for being tough and durable due to their heavy-duty steel frames and high-quality components. Scag mowers are also known for their durability, and their welded steel frames are designed to last for many years.

Warranty: Both Ferris and Scag offer warranties for their mowers. Ferris offers a limited warranty of 4 years or 500 hours, whichever comes first, for their mowers. Scag offers a 3-year limited warranty on their mowers.

Both warranties cover defects in materials and workmanship.

Parts availability: Both brands have a good supply of replacement parts available. Ferris and Scag both have authorized dealers who stock genuine parts and can provide repairs and maintenance services. However, parts for Ferris mowers may be slightly more expensive due to the patented suspension system.

Both Ferris and Scag mowers are durable and require regular maintenance. The warranties offered by both brands are good, and replacement parts are readily available. However, the maintenance required for each brand may differ, and the cost of replacement parts for Ferris mowers may be slightly higher due to the patented suspension system.

Tips to Consider When Choosing Between Ferris and Scag Mowers

Here are a few more tips to consider when choosing between Ferris and Scag mowers:

Test Drive Both Brands

Before making a final decision, it’s important to test drive both Ferris and Scag mowers to get a feel for how they operate. This will give you a better understanding of how the suspension system, deck design, and engine power affect the overall performance of the mower.

Consider Your Terrain

The type of terrain you will be mowing should also play a role in your decision. If you have a lot of hills or uneven terrain, a mower with a suspension system like the Ferris IS 2100Z may be a better option. If you have a flat lawn, a Scag mower without a suspension system may be a more cost-effective choice.

Look for Local Dealers

When purchasing a commercial mower, it’s important to have access to local dealers for maintenance and repairs. Research local dealers for both Ferris and Scag and consider their availability and expertise when making your decision.

Compare Warranties

While both brands offer warranties, it’s important to compare the details of each to understand what is covered and for how long. This will give you a better idea of the long-term cost of ownership for each brand.

Consider Additional Features

Both Ferris and Scag offer additional features that can enhance the performance and comfort of the mower. Consider the availability and cost of features such as bagging systems, mulching kits, and comfort options like padded seats and armrests when making your decision.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are There Any Notable Differences in the Maneuverability of Ferris and Scag Mowers?

Yes, there are some differences in maneuverability between the two brands. Ferris mowers are known for their excellent maneuverability thanks to their independent suspension systems, which allow each wheel to move up and down independently.

This feature allows the mower to better conform to the contours of the ground and navigate tight spaces. Scag mowers, on the other hand, are known for their compact size and tight turning radius, which also contribute to good maneuverability.

What Are Some Common Maintenance Tasks That Should Be Performed on Both Ferris and Scag Mowers?

Both Ferris and Scag mowers require routine maintenance to keep them running smoothly. Some common maintenance tasks include checking and changing the oil, replacing the air filter, checking the spark plugs, and sharpening the blades. It’s also important to regularly inspect the mower for damage and wear and tear, and to make any necessary repairs or replacements.

Can You Use a Ferris or Scag Mower for Residential Lawn Care, or Are They Only Suitable for Commercial Use?

While Ferris and Scag mowers are designed primarily for commercial use, they can certainly be used for residential lawn care as well. However, they may be overkill for smaller lawns, as they are generally larger and more powerful than the average residential mower. Additionally, they are typically more expensive than residential mowers, so they may not be the most cost-effective option for homeowners.

What is the Warranty Offered by Ferris and Scag for Their Mowers?

The warranty offered by Ferris and Scag varies depending on the specific model and components of the mower. Generally, both brands offer a limited warranty that covers defects in materials and workmanship for a certain period of time.

Ferris mowers typically come with a 4-year limited warranty, while Scag mowers come with a 3-year limited warranty. However, it’s important to read the specific warranty information for the mower you are interested in, as there may be variations in coverage.

Do Ferris and Scag offer any additional accessories or attachments for their mowers?

Both Ferris and Scag offer a variety of additional accessories and attachments that can be used with their mowers. For example, both brands offer bagging systems, mulching kits, and striping kits that can be used to enhance the performance and appearance of the mower. Additionally, there are a variety of aftermarket accessories and attachments available from third-party vendors that can be used with Ferris and Scag mowers.

Conclusion

In conclusion, both Ferris and Scag are reputable commercial mower brands, each with their unique features and benefits. In terms of design and features, Ferris stands out for its advanced suspension system, while Scag is known for its heavy-duty construction.

When it comes to cutting performance, both brands offer various deck sizes and designs to suit different needs. However, Ferris’ cutting performance is generally considered to be superior due to its unique iCD cutting system.

In terms of engine options, both brands offer a range of horsepower ratings, and the choice ultimately depends on the specific needs and preferences of the buyer. Price-wise, Scag mowers are generally more affordable than Ferris, but the latter makes up for it with advanced features and performance.

Finally, both brands offer warranties and have readily available parts, but Ferris tends to be slightly more durable in the long run. Ultimately, the choice between Ferris and Scag depends on the specific needs and preferences of the buyer.

While both brands are excellent choices, Ferris may be the better option for those who prioritize advanced features and performance, while Scag may be the more practical choice for those on a budget.
