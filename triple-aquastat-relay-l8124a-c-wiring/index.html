How does a triple aquastat work?

A triple aquastat is a type of temperature control device used in boilers and heating systems to regulate and maintain the temperature of the water. It is called a “triple” aquastat because it has three separate temperature sensors and controls.

Components of Triple Aquastat

The main components of a triple aquastat include a temperature sensing bulb, a control switch, and electrical wiring. The temperature sensing bulb is filled with a heat-sensitive liquid and is connected to the control switch by a tube. As the temperature of the water changes, the liquid in the bulb expands or contracts, causing pressure changes that activate the switch.

Operation of Triple Aquastat

The triple aquastat works by constantly monitoring the temperature of the water in the heating system and controlling the boiler accordingly. There are three separate temperature sensors and controls, each with its own set point, that work together to maintain the desired temperature range.

The first control is the low limit control, which turns the boiler off if the water temperature drops below a certain point. The second control is the high limit control, which turns the boiler off if the water temperature exceeds a certain point. The third control is the circulation control, which ensures that the water is circulating properly through the system.

Advantages of Triple Aquastat

The main advantage of a triple aquastat is its ability to provide precise temperature control and to ensure safe and efficient operation of the heating system. The multiple temperature sensors and controls allow for a wider range of temperature control, reducing the risk of overheating or underheating and preventing damage to the system.
