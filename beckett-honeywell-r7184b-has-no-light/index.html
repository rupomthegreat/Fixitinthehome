Beckett Honeywell R7184B No Light

The Beckett AFG Series with Honeywell R7184B control is a type of oil furnace used to heat a home or business. If the furnace is not working and the reset light is not on, it could be due to a number of issues.

One potential cause could be a problem with the thermostat or its wiring. To troubleshoot this, you can try installing a small jumper between the terminals where the thermostat wire connects. If the burner starts within 15 seconds, it is likely that there is a problem with the thermostat or its wiring.

Another potential cause could be a malfunction or failure in the furnace itself. In this case, it may be necessary to perform a reset of the furnace by holding down the reset button for 30 seconds until the LED light flashes twice.

This should unlock the furnace and allow it to operate again. If the furnace goes into lock out mode, it is likely that there is a problem that needs to be addressed.

Why is My Oil Burner Not Igniting?

Blocked Oil Filter

  1. Causes: Clogging in the oil filter will prevent oil from flowing to the burner.
  2. Fix: Replace the oil filter and clean the oil lines.

Faulty Coil

  1. Causes: A faulty ignition coil will prevent the oil burner from igniting.
  2. Fix: Replace the ignition coil.

Incorrectly Set Electrodes

  1. Causes: Incorrectly set electrodes will prevent the spark from reaching the oil.
  2. Fix: Adjust the electrodes to the proper position.

Blockage in Nozzle Hosing

  1. Causes: Blockages in the nozzle hosing will prevent the oil from reaching the burner.
  2. Fix: Clean or replace the nozzle hosing.

Insufficient Air Flow

  1. Causes: A lack of air flow to the burner will prevent it from igniting.
  2. Fix: Check the air intake and ducts for any blockages, and clean or replace if necessary. Ensure that the air flow is sufficient for proper combustion.
