What Does 1 Flash Mean on a Goodman Furnace? How to Fix?

Edited byMinhazul Abedin February 7, 2023January 3, 2025

Furnaces are essential appliances that provide heat and warmth during the colder months of the year. However, like any mechanical device, furnaces can experience problems from time to time. One common issue that homeowners may encounter is the furnace not igniting. This can be frustrating and potentially leave your home without heat.

Often, the problem can be indicated by a specific error code, such as the one blink error code on a Goodman furnace. If your Goodman furnace is experiencing this issue, it is important to understand the possible causes. Take steps to diagnose and repair the problem.

In this article, we will explore the one blink error code on a Goodman furnace. We will also provide troubleshooting tips to help you get your furnace back up and running smoothly.

Goodman Furnace Won’t Ignite 1 Blink

If your Goodman furnace is not igniting and is displaying a one blink error code, it is likely that the furnace is in lockout mode. This means that the furnace has detected a problem and has shut down to prevent further damage.

To reset the furnace, you will need to turn off the power and then turn it back on after a few seconds. If the problem persists, you will need to diagnose the issue further. This will help determine the cause.

Possible causes of a Goodman furnace not igniting with a one blink error code include a faulty ignition control board, a faulty thermostat, a malfunctioning hot surface igniter, a clogged air filter, or a malfunctioning flame sensor.

You may need to perform some basic troubleshooting steps. These include checking the thermostat settings, checking the air filter, or cleaning the flame sensor. These steps can help determine the root cause of the issue. If you are unable to diagnose the problem on your own, you should consider calling a professional HVAC technician. They can diagnose and repair the issue.

Potential Causes of a Goodman Furnace Not Igniting

There are several potential causes of a Goodman furnace not igniting, including:

Faulty Ignition Control Board

The ignition control board is responsible for controlling the ignition process. It ensures that the furnace starts up properly. If the ignition control board is faulty, it can prevent the furnace from igniting.

Faulty Thermostat

The thermostat sends a signal to the furnace to start heating when the temperature inside the home drops below the set point. If the thermostat is not functioning properly, it may not send the proper signal to the furnace. This can cause it to fail to ignite.

Malfunctioning Hot Surface Igniter

The hot surface igniter is responsible for generating the spark that ignites the gas in the furnace. If the hot surface igniter is not functioning properly, the furnace will not ignite.

Clogged Air Filter

A clogged air filter can restrict the flow of air to the furnace. This can cause it to overheat and shut down. It can also prevent the furnace from igniting.

Malfunctioning Flame Sensor

The flame sensor is responsible for detecting the presence of a flame in the furnace. If the flame sensor is not functioning properly, it may prevent the furnace from igniting. It may also cause it to shut down unexpectedly.

Any of these issues can cause a Goodman furnace not to ignite. To determine the root cause of the problem, you will need to perform some basic troubleshooting steps. If necessary, consult with a professional HVAC technician.

Troubleshooting Steps

There are a few basic troubleshooting steps that you can try if your Goodman furnace is not igniting:

Check Thermostat Settings

Make sure that the thermostat is set to “heat” and that the temperature is set to a comfortable level. If the thermostat is not set correctly, it may not send the proper signal to the furnace. This can prevent it from starting heating.

Check and Replace the Air Filter

A clogged air filter can restrict the flow of air to the furnace. This can cause it to overheat and shut down. Check the air filter and replace it if it is dirty or clogged.

Clean the Flame Sensor

The flame sensor is responsible for detecting the presence of a flame in the furnace. If the flame sensor is dirty or covered in debris, it may not function properly. Clean the flame sensor by gently brushing it with a soft cloth or a small brush.

If these troubleshooting steps do not resolve the issue, it may be necessary to call a professional HVAC technician. They can diagnose and repair the problem.
