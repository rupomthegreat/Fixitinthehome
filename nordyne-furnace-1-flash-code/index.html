Nordyne Furnace 1 Flash Code [Meaning, Causes, Fixes]

Edited byMinhazul Abedin January 17, 2023January 17, 2023

Nordyne furnaces are a popular choice for home heating systems, known for their reliability and efficiency. However, like any mechanical system, Nordyne furnaces can sometimes experience issues. One common problem that homeowners may encounter is a 1 flash error code.

This error code can indicate a problem with the limit circuit or an external load on the “W” terminal, and it can be caused by a variety of factors. In this article, we will explore the causes and potential solutions for a Nordyne furnace 1 flash error code.

Nordyne Furnace 1 Flash Code

A Nordyne furnace may display a 1 flash error code when there is an issue with the limit circuit or an external load on the “W” terminal.

1 flash error code indicates that there is an open circuit or an overload in the system, and it may be caused by a variety of issues such as a faulty thermostat, a malfunctioning blower motor, or a damaged heat exchanger.

To troubleshoot this issue, it may be necessary to check the thermostat and blower motor for proper operation, clean or replace the air filter, and inspect the heat exchanger for cracks or other damage.

If these steps do not resolve the issue, it is recommended to contact a professional HVAC technician for further assistance.

What Is A 1 Flash Error Code?

A Nordyne furnace 1 flash error code indicates that there is an issue with the limit circuit or an external load on the “W” terminal. The limit circuit is a safety feature that is designed to prevent the furnace from overheating.

It consists of a sensor and a switch that monitors the temperature inside the furnace and shuts off the furnace if the temperature exceeds a certain level. An external load on the “W” terminal, on the other hand, refers to any additional electrical load placed on the terminal, such as a thermostat or blower motor, which may cause an overload in the system.

The 1 flash error code may be caused by a variety of issues, including a faulty thermostat, a malfunctioning blower motor, or a damaged heat exchanger. It is important to identify and address the root cause of the issue in order to prevent further problems with the furnace.

In some cases, the error code may be caused by a simple issue that can be easily resolved, such as a dirty air filter or a tripped circuit breaker. However, it is always recommended to consult with a professional HVAC technician if you are unsure of the cause or if you are unable to resolve the issue on your own.

How To Troubleshoot A 1 Flash Error Code

If you are experiencing a Nordyne furnace 1 flash error code, there are a few steps you can take to troubleshoot the issue:

Check The Thermostat

Make sure that the thermostat is set to “Heat” and that the temperature is set higher than the current room temperature. If the thermostat is not functioning properly, it may prevent the furnace from turning on.

Check The Air Filter

A dirty air filter can restrict airflow and cause the furnace to overheat. Replace the air filter if it is dirty or clogged.

Check The Blower Motor

The blower motor is responsible for circulating air through the furnace. If the blower motor is not functioning properly, it may cause the limit circuit to trip. Check the blower motor and replace it if necessary.

Check The Heat Exchanger

The heat exchanger is the component of the furnace that generates heat. If the heat exchanger is damaged or has a crack, it may cause the limit circuit to trip. Inspect the heat exchanger for any visible damage and replace it if necessary.

Reset The Limit Switch

If the limit switch has tripped, it may be necessary to reset it. Locate the switch and follow the manufacturer’s instructions for resetting it.

Proper maintenance and cleaning of your Nordyne furnace is crucial for ensuring that it operates efficiently and safely.

In addition to checking and replacing the air filter regularly, it is recommended to have a professional HVAC technician inspect and service your furnace on an annual basis. This can help to identify and resolve any potential issues before they become serious problems.

Common Causes Of A 1 Flash Error Code

There are several common causes of a Nordyne furnace 1 flash error code, including:

Faulty Thermostat

If the thermostat is not functioning properly, it may prevent the furnace from turning on or may cause the limit circuit to trip. To troubleshoot this issue, check the thermostat settings and replace the thermostat if necessary.

Malfunctioning Blower Motor

The blower motor is responsible for circulating air through the furnace. If the blower motor is not functioning properly, it may cause the limit circuit to trip. Check the blower motor and replace it if necessary.

Damaged Heat Exchanger

The heat exchanger is the component of the furnace that generates heat. If the heat exchanger is damaged or has a crack, it may cause the limit circuit to trip. Inspect the heat exchanger for any visible damage and replace it if necessary.

To identify and address these issues, it is important to follow the troubleshooting steps outlined in the previous section and consult with a professional HVAC technician if necessary. Proper maintenance and cleaning of your Nordyne furnace can also help to prevent issues from occurring in the first place.

In addition to these common causes, it is also possible that the 1 flash error code may be caused by a problem with the furnace’s control board or other electrical components. If you are unable to identify the root cause of the issue, it is recommended to contact a professional HVAC technician for further assistance.

Nordyne Furnace Red Light Blinking 2 Times

The red light blinking 2 times on a Nordyne furnace typically indicates a pressure switch issue. Causes of this issue can include a clogged exhaust pipe or a malfunctioning blower.

Limit switch is open

The limit switch is a safety device that monitors the temperature of the furnace. If the limit switch is open, it means that the furnace is overheating and needs to shut down. The fix for this is to check the air filter and make sure it is clean, check the blower motor to make sure it is functioning properly, and check the ductwork to make sure there is proper airflow.

Pressure switch is open

The pressure switch is a safety device that monitors the pressure in the furnace’s combustion chamber. If the pressure switch is open, it means that there is not enough airflow in the furnace. The fix for this is to check the air filter and make sure it is clean, check the blower motor to make sure it is functioning properly, and check the exhaust pipe to make sure it is not clogged.

Ignition control module is faulty

The ignition control module is responsible for controlling the ignition process of the furnace. If it is faulty, the furnace will not be able to ignite and will not function properly. The fix for this is to replace the ignition control module.

Thermostat is malfunctioning

The thermostat controls the temperature of the furnace. If it is malfunctioning, the furnace may not turn on or may not reach the desired temperature. The fix for this is to check the thermostat’s settings and make sure they are correct, and if necessary, replace the thermostat.
