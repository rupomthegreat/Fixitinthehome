Bi-fold Door Rough Opening Size for 72 Inch [Calculation, Step-by-Step Guide]

Installing a bifold door can be a great way to create an open, airy feel in your home, but it’s important to get the rough opening just right. A rough opening that’s too small can make it difficult to install the door properly, while one that’s too big can leave gaps and affect the door’s operation.

If you’re planning to install a 72 inch bifold door, you’ll need to ensure that the rough opening is 2 inches wider and 2 inches taller than the actual door size.

In this article, we’ll explore the ins and outs of rough openings for bifold doors and give you everything you need to know to ensure a smooth and successful installation.

Importance of 72 Rough Opening Size

One of the key reasons why it’s important to have the right rough opening size for a 72 inch bifold door is to accommodate the door’s hardware. Bifold doors come with a variety of different hardware components, including hinges, tracks, rollers, and locking mechanisms, and these components need enough space to be installed properly.

The rough opening for a bi-fold door should be 2 inches wider and 2 inches taller than the actual door size. This allows for proper installation of the door and its hardware and ensures that the door operates smoothly and efficiently.

When measuring the rough opening, it’s important to make sure that it is level, square, and supported by sturdy wall framing. By following these guidelines, you’ll be able to ensure that your door is installed correctly and functions as it should.

Ensuring Proper Door Operation

Another important reason for having the right rough opening size for a 72 inch bifold door is to ensure proper door operation. If the rough opening is not level or square, or if the wall framing is not sturdy enough, the door may not operate smoothly and may even become damaged over time. By having a rough opening that’s the right size, you’ll ensure that the door operates smoothly and efficiently, and you’ll avoid any potential damage or wear and tear.

Avoiding Damage to the Door

Finally, having the right rough opening size for a 72 inch bifold door is important to avoid damage to the door itself. If the rough opening is too small, the door may become jammed or stuck, and if it’s too big, it can leave gaps that can allow air and light to enter the room. By having the right rough opening size, you’ll ensure that the door fits properly and is protected from damage, and you’ll be able to enjoy the benefits of your new bifold door for years to come.

Rough Opening Size for a 72 Inch Bifold Door

The standard rough opening size for a 72 inch bifold door is 74 inches wide and 82 inches tall. This means that the opening in the wall where the door will be installed should be 2 inches wider and 2 inches taller than the actual door size. The extra width and height are necessary to accommodate the door’s hardware and to ensure that the door can be installed and operates properly.

Explanation of 2 Inch Increase in Width and Height

The 2 inch increase in width and height is necessary to accommodate the door’s hardware and to ensure that the door can be installed and operates properly. The hinges, tracks, rollers, and locking mechanisms all need enough space to be installed correctly, and the extra width and height provide this space.

In addition, the extra width and height help to ensure that the door operates smoothly and does not become damaged over time.

Step by Step Calculation

  1. Measure the actual door size: The first step is to measure the actual size of the bifold door that you are planning to install. This measurement should be taken at the widest and tallest points of the door.
  2. Add 2 inches to the width: Once you have the actual door size, you’ll need to add 2 inches to the width to determine the rough opening width. For example, if the actual door size is 72 inches, the rough opening width will be 74 inches.
  3. Add 2 inches to the height: Next, add 2 inches to the height of the actual door size to determine the rough opening height. For example, if the actual door size is 80 inches, the rough opening height will be 82 inches.
  4. Record the rough opening dimensions: Record the rough opening dimensions by writing down the width and height, such as 74 inches wide and 82 inches tall.
  5. Check for level and square: Before installing the bifold door, it’s important to check the rough opening to make sure it is level and square. You can do this by measuring the width and height of the opening at several points and making sure that the measurements are consistent.
  6. Check the wall framing: Finally, make sure that the wall framing is sturdy enough to support the weight of the door. If the wall framing is not strong enough, reinforce it before installing the door.

By following these simple steps, you’ll be able to determine the rough opening size for a 72 inch bifold door and ensure a smooth and successful installation.
