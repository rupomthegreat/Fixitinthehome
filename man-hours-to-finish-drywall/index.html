How Much Man Hours is Needed to Finish Drywall Installation?

Edited byMinhazul Abedin January 9, 2023January 9, 2023

The process of installing drywall, also known as wallboard or plasterboard, is a common element of construction and home renovation projects. It involves hanging the drywall sheets on the wall or ceiling, and then taping and finishing the seams and corners to create a smooth and seamless surface.

Determining the number of man-hours required to finish a drywall installation project can help contractors and homeowners plan for the labor and budget needed for the job.

On average, it takes professional drywall hangers and finishers 2 to 3 days to complete a 1000 sq ft drywall installation project, but the actual time needed may vary based on the complexity of the space and the skill level of the workers.

Factors That Affect Man Hours to Finish Drywall Installation

There are several factors that can affect man hours needed for drywall installation including the size of the area, the number of openings and corners, the skill of the workers, and the material used. But on average it might take about 200 hours for 1000 sq. ft. of drywall installation.

Size of the area

The larger the area, the more time it will take to hang and finish the drywall. This is because there will be more sheets of drywall to hang, more seams to tape and finish, and more surfaces to sand and touch up.

Number of openings and corners

The more openings and corners in a space, the longer it will take to install drywall. These areas require additional cutting and fitting of the drywall sheets, as well as additional taping and finishing to create smooth transitions.

Skill level of the workers

Experienced drywall hangers and finishers will be able to complete a job more quickly than those who are less experienced. This is because they will have a better understanding of the proper techniques and will be able to work more efficiently.

Type of materials used

The type of drywall and joint compound used can also affect the man hours needed for a drywall project. Using quicker-drying materials can help to reduce the time needed between coats, while using higher-quality materials may result in a smoother finish that requires less sanding and touch-up work.

Average Man Hours For Professional Drywall Installation

Average man hours for professional drywall installation:

  • Hanging drywall: Professional drywall hangers can typically hang the drywall sheets for a 1000 sq ft area in 1/2 day to 1 day, depending on the complexity of the space and the skill level of the workers.
  • Tapping and finishing: Tapping and finishing the drywall seams and corners typically take 1 to 2 days for a 1000 sq ft area. This includes applying multiple coats of joint compound, allowing the compound to dry between coats, and sanding and touching up the finished surface.
  • Total: On average, it takes 2 to 3 days for professional drywall hangers and finishers to complete a 1000 sq ft drywall installation project.

It’s important to note that these are just estimates and the actual time needed may vary based on the specific project and the factors mentioned above. It’s always a good idea to allow extra time for unexpected issues or delays that may come up during the drywall installation process.

DIY Man Hours For Drywall Installation

DIY man hours for drywall installation:

  • Hanging drywall: For a DIYer or someone with less experience, hanging drywall for a 1000 sq ft area may take several days. This is because it may take longer to accurately measure, cut, and fit the drywall sheets, and there may be more mistakes or issues that require additional time to correct.
  • Tapping and finishing: Tapping and finishing the drywall seams and corners may take up to a week for a DIYer to complete. This is because it may take longer to apply and smooth the joint compound, and there may be more sanding and touch-up work needed to achieve a smooth finish.
  • Total: For a DIYer, it may take several days to a week to complete a drywall installation project for a 1000 sq ft area.

It’s important to note that these are just estimates and the actual time needed may vary based on the specific project and the factors mentioned above. It’s always a good idea to allow extra time for unexpected issues or delays that may come up during the drywall installation process.

Additionally, hiring a professional drywall contractor may be a good option for those who want to complete a drywall project more quickly and with higher-quality results.

DIY installation vs Professional installation

The time it takes to hang and finish drywall can vary greatly depending on a number of factors, including the size of the area, the number of openings and corners, the skill level of the workers, and the type of materials being used.

Generally, professional drywall hangers and finishers can complete a 1000 sq ft area in a few days, while it may take a DIYer or someone with less experience several days or even a week to complete the same job. It’s always a good idea to allow extra time for unexpected issues or delays that may come up during the drywall installation process.

There are pros and cons to both DIY drywall installation and professional installation. Here is a comparison of the two options:

DIY Installation

Pros:

  • Can save money on labor costs
  • Can be a sense of accomplishment to do the work oneself
  • Allows for customization and personal touch

Cons:

  • Requires knowledge and skill to properly install drywall
  • Can be time-consuming and physically demanding
  • Can be more prone to mistakes and imperfections

Professional Installation

Pros:

  • Can be completed more quickly and efficiently
  • Professionals have the knowledge and experience to ensure a high-quality result
  • May come with a warranty or guarantee for their work

Cons:

  • Can be more expensive due to labor costs
  • Limited ability to customize or add personal touches

Tips For Reducing Man Hours On Drywall Projects

Tips for reducing man hours on drywall projects:

Planning ahead and gathering all necessary materials

Having all of the materials and tools needed for the job before starting can help to reduce the time spent on the project. This includes not only the drywall sheets and joint compound, but also items such as a drywall lift, T-squares, and sanding equipment.

Having everything ready to go will allow the work to progress smoothly without delays caused by having to stop and gather more supplies. Also, consider clean and mold-free supplies.

Working efficiently and effectively as a team

Having a team of workers who are able to work well together and divide tasks effectively can help to reduce the overall man hours needed for the project. By assigning tasks and working efficiently, it’s possible to complete the job more quickly and with fewer mistakes.

Using quick-drying materials

Using quick-drying joint compound and other materials can help to reduce the time needed between coats, allowing the project to be completed more quickly. This is especially useful for projects that are on a tight timeline or where multiple coats are required.

By considering these factors and implementing strategies to reduce man hours, it’s possible to complete a drywall project more efficiently and within budget. You shouldn’t cover everything with drywall.

Conclusion

It’s important to consider man hours when budgeting for drywall projects as it can have a significant impact on the overall cost and timeline of the project. There are several factors that can affect the man hours needed for drywall installation, including the size of the area, the number of openings and corners, the skill level of the workers, and the type of materials used.

On average, it takes professional drywall hangers and finishers 2 to 3 days to complete a 1000 sq ft drywall installation project, while it may take a DIYer or someone with less experience several days to a week to complete the same job.

It’s also important to consider the value of professional drywall installation services, as they can provide efficient and high-quality results. Professional contractors have the experience, skills, and tools necessary to complete a drywall project quickly and with minimal mistakes, which can save time and money in the long run.

By hiring a professional drywall contractor, it’s possible to complete a drywall project with confidence, knowing that the job will be done right and on schedule.
