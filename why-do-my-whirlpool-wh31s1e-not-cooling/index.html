Why Do My Whirlpool WH31S1E Not Cooling? [How to Fix?]

The Whirlpool WH31S1E refrigerator is a reliable and convenient appliance that is commonly found in households around the world. However, like any other appliance, it can experience problems from time to time.

One common issue that people often encounter with their Whirlpool WH31S1E fridge is that it is not cooling properly. If you find that your fridge is not maintaining a proper temperature, it can be frustrating and inconvenient.

In this article, we will explore some possible causes for this problem and provide some troubleshooting steps that you can try to fix it.

Whirlpool Wh31s1e Not Cooling, Things You Need to Do

If your Whirlpool WH31S1E refrigerator is not cooling, there could be a few possible causes. Here are some steps you can take to troubleshoot the issue:

Check the Thermostat

Make sure the thermostat is turned to the correct setting and that it is functioning properly. You should hear a click when you turn the thermostat from OFF to maximum cold. If you don’t hear a click, the thermostat may be defective.

Check the Compressor

Make sure the compressor is running and not overheating. If the compressor is running but not cooling, it may be malfunctioning.

Check the Compressor Wiring

Make sure the compressor wiring is connected properly and not damaged.

Check the Evaporator Coils

If the evaporator coils are dirty or frosted over, it can prevent the refrigerator from cooling properly.

If you’ve checked these things and the refrigerator is still not cooling, it may be necessary to call a repair technician for further assistance. It could be a more serious issue, such as a malfunctioning compressor or a problem with the refrigerant.

Possible Causes of the Fridge Not Cooling

Thermostat Not Set Correctly

One potential cause of a fridge not cooling properly is a thermostat that is not set correctly or that is malfunctioning. The thermostat is responsible for regulating the temperature inside the fridge and freezer.

Thermostat Functioning Properly

If it is not set to the correct temperature or if it is not functioning properly, the fridge will not be able to maintain a consistent temperature. It is important to check the thermostat and make sure it is set to the correct temperature and that it is functioning properly.

Defective Thermostat

If you turn the thermostat from OFF to maximum cold and you do not hear a click, it could be a sign that the thermostat is defective. In this case, you may need to replace the thermostat in order to fix the problem.

Troubleshooting Steps

Another troubleshooting step to try when a fridge is not cooling is to check the compressor. The compressor is a vital part of the fridge’s cooling system and is responsible for pumping refrigerant through the system to cool the air inside the fridge.

If the compressor is not running or if it is malfunctioning, the fridge will not be able to maintain a proper temperature. To check the compressor, you will need to pull out the fridge and locate the compressor at the back of the unit.

Make sure the compressor is running and not overheating. If the compressor is not running or if it feels excessively hot to the touch, it could be a sign that the compressor is malfunctioning. In this case, you may need to call a repair technician for further assistance.

Why is My Refrigerator Working but Not Cooling?

It is important to diagnose and fix the root cause of a refrigerator not cooling to ensure it functions efficiently and effectively. Regular maintenance, such as cleaning the condenser coils and checking the door seals, can help to prevent problems from occurring and ensure your refrigerator runs smoothly for years to come.

Dirty Condenser Coils

The condenser coils in the refrigerator play a crucial role in dissipating heat and maintaining a cool temperature inside the unit. Dirty condenser coils reduce the efficiency of the coils, leading to a build-up of heat inside the refrigerator and preventing it from cooling.

Thermostat Issues

The thermostat controls the temperature inside the refrigerator by switching the cooling system on and off. If the thermostat is faulty, it can cause the refrigerator to stop cooling or not cool to the desired temperature.

Evaporator Fan Failure

The evaporator fan circulates the air inside the refrigerator to maintain a consistent temperature. If the fan fails, the air inside the refrigerator will not circulate, leading to uneven cooling and potentially causing some parts of the refrigerator to become warm.

Defrosting Problems

The defrosting mechanism in a refrigerator helps to melt any frost that forms on the coils, which can restrict the flow of air and reduce the efficiency of the cooling system. If the defrosting mechanism fails, it can cause the refrigerator to stop cooling or cool inefficiently.

Leaky Door Seals

The door seals play a crucial role in maintaining a constant temperature inside the refrigerator by preventing cold air from escaping. If the door seals are damaged or leaky, cold air will escape, and the refrigerator will not cool efficiently.

Compressor Problems

The compressor is responsible for compressing the refrigerant and distributing it to the condenser coils, where it dissipates heat. If the compressor fails, the refrigerant will not circulate, and the refrigerator will not cool.

Power Supply Issues

If the refrigerator is not receiving power or is connected to an outlet that is not working, it will not function properly. Power outages, tripped breakers, and frayed power cords can all cause the refrigerator to stop working or not cool properly.

How Do I Reset My Whirlpool Refrigerator Not Cooling?

Step 1: Locate the Buttons Find the “filter” and “smart grid” buttons on your Whirlpool refrigerator.

Step 2: Press the Buttons Press and hold both the “filter” and “smart grid” buttons at the same time for about 3 seconds.

Step 3: Check Cooling Function Check if the cooling function of your Whirlpool refrigerator is now back on.

Step 4: Monitor Temperature Monitor the temperature inside the refrigerator to ensure it is cooling properly.

Note: If this does not solve the issue, it is recommended to seek assistance from a professional or refer to the user manual for further troubleshooting steps.

Prevention of Such Issue

Here are a few additional tips to consider if your Whirlpool WH31S1E fridge is not cooling properly:

Check the Door Seals

Make sure the door seals are clean and in good condition. If the seals are dirty or damaged, they may not be sealing properly, which can cause the fridge to not maintain a proper temperature.

Make Sure the Fridge is Level

If the fridge is not level, it can cause the cooling system to work less efficiently. Use a level to check that the fridge is properly balanced and adjust the feet if necessary.

Clean the Condenser Coils

The condenser coils are responsible for dissipating heat from the fridge. If the coils are dirty or clogged, it can cause the fridge to not cool properly. Use a brush or vacuum to clean the coils regularly.

Check for Air Flow Blockages

Make sure there is proper air flow around the fridge. If there are items blocking the air flow, it can cause the fridge to not cool properly.

Check the Temperature Setting

Make sure the temperature setting on the fridge is not set too low. If the temperature is set too low, it can cause the fridge to run constantly, which can lead to issues with cooling.

Check the Power Supply

Make sure the fridge is plugged in and that the outlet is functioning properly. If the fridge is not receiving power, it will not be able to run and cool properly.

What to Do if These Troubleshooting Steps Don’t Work

If you have tried the troubleshooting steps outlined above and your fridge is still not cooling, it may be necessary to call a repair technician for further assistance. There could be a more serious issue at play, such as a malfunctioning compressor or a problem with the refrigerant.

A qualified repair technician will be able to diagnose the issue and recommend the appropriate repair or replacement. While it may be tempting to try to fix the problem yourself, it is important to remember that refrigerators contain complex mechanical and electrical components that can be dangerous to work on if you are not properly trained.

It is always best to err on the side of caution and call a professional if you are unable to fix the problem on your own. Also careful about splicing Romex wires.

Conclusion

In conclusion, if your Whirlpool WH31S1E fridge is not cooling properly, there could be a few possible causes. These include a thermostat that is not set correctly or malfunctioning, a compressor that is not running or malfunctioning, compressor wiring issues, or dirty or frosted evaporator coils.

By following the troubleshooting steps outlined in this article, you may be able to fix the problem on your own and save time and money by not having to call a repair technician. However, if you are unable to fix the issue after trying these steps, it is important to call a professional for further assistance.

They will be able to diagnose the problem and recommend the appropriate repair or replacement.
