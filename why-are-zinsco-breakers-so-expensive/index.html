Zinsco Breakers Aren’t Safe

Zinsco breakers are one of the most commonly used electrical components in homes and businesses.

However, they are not safe to use and may fail more often than safer options. This can lead to dangerous situations where you could be without power or unable to access your home or business.

They Fail More Consistently Than Safe

Zinsco breakers are known for their susceptibility to failure. When these units fail, it is usually due to a number of factors including corrosion, abuse, and inadequate installation procedures. As a result, it is important that you replace them when they begin to fail rather than trying to fix them – this will typically be more expensive in the long run.

They Are Dangerous To Use

When broken, zinsco breakers can cause serious injury if touched or tripped over – especially if children are nearby. In addition, these devices pose a fire hazard because they contain flammable materials within their construction (such as propane gas). Finally, breaking an unprotected breaker panel can damage other parts of your electrical system which may require costly repairs down the road.

If you need to replace a zinsco breaker panel in your home or business then expect costs associated with this repair project to reach well into the hundreds of dollars range. It depends on the size and complexity of your installation.

What is wrong with Zinsco breakers?

There are several design flaws with Zinsco breakers that can lead to problems when using them in your home. Rust and wiring issues can cause the panels to overheat, interfering with electricity flow and potentially causing a fire.

Breakers can be faulty, leading to an overload or interference in the electrical system as well as damage to other components of the breaker box. Are Zinsco panels Legal?
