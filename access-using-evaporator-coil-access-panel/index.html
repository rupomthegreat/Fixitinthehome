How to Access Using Evaporator Coil Access Panel?

Edited byMinhazul Abedin February 8, 2023February 11, 2023

An evaporator coil is a vital component of a home’s heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) system. It is responsible for absorbing heat from the air inside the home, and is typically located inside the air handler or furnace.

Regular maintenance and cleaning of the evaporator coil is important to ensure that it is functioning properly and efficiently. This often involves accessing the evaporator coil through an access panel, which may be located in various locations depending on the type and model of the HVAC system.

In this article, we will discuss the steps for accessing and cleaning the evaporator coil, as well as when it is best to call a professional for assistance.

Accessing the Evaporator Coil

To access the evaporator coil, the first step is to locate the access panel. This panel is usually located on the side or top of the air handler or furnace and is typically secured with screws or clips. In some cases, the access panel may be hidden behind a panel or piece of insulation, so it may be necessary to remove these materials to locate it.

One common challenge in accessing the evaporator coil is limited visibility. This can be due to the location of the access panel or the presence of other components that obstruct the view of the coil. In these cases, it may be helpful to use a flashlight or other light source to better see the coil and any attached components.

Another challenge that may arise is if the access panel is sealed shut. This can be due to the panel being old or having been sealed with adhesives or other materials. In these cases, it may be necessary to cut a new access panel in order to gain access to the evaporator coil.

This should only be done as a last resort and with caution, as it can be a complex and potentially dangerous task. It is usually best to hire a trained and certified HVAC technician to cut a new access panel if needed.

Is It Okay to Use the Access Panel?

It is generally not recommended for homeowners to try to clean their evaporator coils themselves as it can be a complex and potentially dangerous task. The evaporator coils are often located inside the air handler or furnace and can be difficult to access.

In some cases, it may be necessary to cut a panel in order to access the coils. It is also important to be careful when handling the coils as they can be fragile and easily damaged. Additionally, if the coils are not handled properly, it can lead to refrigerant leaks, which can be dangerous and require the assistance of a professional to fix.

It is usually best to hire a trained and certified HVAC technician to clean the evaporator coils as they have the knowledge, experience, and tools to do the job safely and effectively.

Cleaning the Evaporator Coil

To clean the evaporator coil, you will need a few tools and materials. These may include:

  1. Soft brush: A soft brush can be used to gently remove dust and debris from the evaporator coil.
  2. Soft cloth: A soft cloth can be used to wipe down the coil after brushing or to absorb any liquids that may be used during the cleaning process.
  3. Air compressor or can of compressed air: Compressed air can be used to blow dust and debris off of the evaporator coil.
  4. Shop vacuum: A shop vacuum can be used to remove dirt and debris from the coil.
  5. Self-cleaning agents: There are specialized cleaning agents available that can be used to help remove dirt and grime from the evaporator coil. These should be used according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

It is important to take safety precautions when cleaning the evaporator coil. The evaporator coil is typically connected to the refrigerant system, which can be dangerous if handled improperly. Be sure to turn off the power to the HVAC system before attempting to clean the evaporator coil. You might need electrical permit for changing something.

Additionally, be careful not to damage the coils, as they are fragile and can easily be bent or broken.

To clean the evaporator coil, follow these steps:

  1. Locate the access panel and remove it to gain access to the evaporator coil.
  2. Use a soft brush to gently remove dust and debris from the coil.
  3. Use a soft cloth or compressed air to wipe down the coil and remove any remaining dirt and debris.
  4. If the coil is heavily soiled, you may need to use a self-cleaning agent according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  5. Use a shop vacuum or other means to remove any debris or cleaning agent that may have accumulated in the air handler or furnace.
  6. Replace the access panel and secure it in place.
  7. Turn the power to the HVAC system back on and test the system to ensure that it is functioning properly.

It is important to be careful and thorough when cleaning the evaporator coil to ensure that it is functioning optimally and efficiently. If the coil is heavily soiled or if there are any issues with the refrigerant system, it is usually best to hire a trained and certified HVAC technician to handle the cleaning and maintenance.

When to Call a Professional

There are certain situations in which it is best to hire a trained and certified HVAC technician to clean the evaporator coil. These may include:

If the Coil is Heavily Soiled

If the evaporator coil is heavily soiled or has not been cleaned in a long time, it may be best to hire a professional to handle the cleaning. This is because the coil may be difficult to access or may require specialized cleaning agents or techniques to effectively remove dirt and grime.

If There Are Any Issues With the Refrigerant System

The evaporator coil is connected to the refrigerant system, and if there are any issues with the refrigerant, it is important to have a professional handle the cleaning and maintenance. This is because handling refrigerant can be dangerous if not done properly, and a professional will have the knowledge and experience to do the job safely and effectively.

If You Are Not Comfortable or Experienced With Hvac Maintenance

If you are not comfortable or experienced with HVAC maintenance, it is usually best to hire a professional to clean the evaporator coil. This will ensure that the job is done safely and efficiently, and can help prevent any potential issues with the HVAC system.

It is also important to note that proper handling and disposal of refrigerant is crucial when cleaning the evaporator coil. Refrigerant can be dangerous if not handled properly, and it is important to follow all safety guidelines and regulations when handling and disposing of it. A professional HVAC technician will have the necessary knowledge and experience to properly handle and dispose of refrigerant.

Conclusion

Maintaining and cleaning the evaporator coil is an important part of ensuring that a home’s HVAC system is functioning properly and efficiently. Accessing the evaporator coil through the access panel is often necessary in order to perform this maintenance, but it can be a complex and potentially dangerous task.

It is important to be cautious and professional when handling the evaporator coil to ensure safety and efficiency. If you are not comfortable or experienced with HVAC maintenance, it is usually best to hire a trained and certified HVAC technician to handle the cleaning and maintenance of the evaporator coil.

By properly maintaining the evaporator coil, you can help ensure that your HVAC system is functioning optimally and efficiently, saving you money on energy costs and prolonging the life of your system.
